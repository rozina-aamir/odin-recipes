Lasagna

Description

A lasagna (also known as a lasagne) is a layered pasta dish that uses wide and flat pasta sheets.

Other contents of a lasagna include meat, white lasagna sauce, and cheese.

This recipe tells you how to create a delicious lasagna from scratch.


Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 750g lean beef mince
  • 90g pack prosciutto
  • 800g passata sauce
  • 200ml hot beef stock
  • nutmeg
  • 300g fresh lasagna sheets
  • white sauce
  • 125g ball mozzarella torn into thin strips

Steps

  1. To make the meat sauce, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a frying pan and cook 750g lean beef mince in two batches for approximately 10 minutes until it's browned all over.
  2. Finely chop 4 slices of proscuitto from a 90g pack, then stir through the meat mixture.
  3. Pour over 800g passata sauce and 200ml hot beef stock. Add some grated nutmeg, then season.
  4. Bring up to the boil, then simmer for 30 minutes until the sauce looks rich.
  5. Heat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4 and lightly oil an ovenproof dish (about 30 x 20cm).
  6. Spoon one third of the meat sauce into the dish, then cover with some fresh lasagne sheets from a 300g pack. Drizzle over roughly 130g ready made white sauce.
  7. Repeat until you have three layers of pasta. Cover with the remaining 390g white sauce, making sure there is no pasta poking through.
  8. Scatter 125g torn mozzarella over the top.
  9. Arrange the rest of the prosciutto on top. Bake for 45 mins until the top is bubbling and lightly browned.
